About

Swatch Internet Time (or beat time) is a decimal time concept introduced in 1998 and marketed by the Swatch corporation as an alternative, decimal measure of time.

One of the goals was to simplify the way people in different time zones communicate about time, mostly by eliminating time zones altogether.

Beats

Instead of hours and minutes, the mean solar day is divided up into 1000 parts called ".beats". Each .beat lasts 1 minute and 26.4 seconds.

Time Zones

There is only a single timezone, equivalent to UTC+1 (Central European Time).

Like UTC, Swatch Internet Time is the same throughout the world, and it does not observe daylight saving time.

Notation

"@248" indicates a time 248 .beats after midnight, equivalent to a fractional day of 0.248 CET, or 4:57:07.2 UTC.

The format of dates used on this site is using the Gregorian calendar in the order day-month-year, separated by periods and prefixed by the letter d (#@beat_date)

More information can be found at Swatch Internet Time on Wikipedia
